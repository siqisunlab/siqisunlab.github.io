About

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis. My research focuses on multimodal medical AI, causal inference, and medical imaging. I develop machine learning methods for real-world clinical data, with an emphasis on clinically meaningful prediction and treatment effect estimation.

My current work studies multimodal prediction and causal inference using electronic health records and optical coherence tomography in ophthalmology. In particular, I investigate whether foundation model based OCT representations provide incremental prognostic and causal value beyond structured clinical data in diabetic macular edema. Before joining WashU, I conducted research in causal learning and medical imaging at Fudan University.
