About

I am a Postdoctoral Associate at Washington University School of Medicine. My research focuses on multimodal causal inference, causal learning, and AI-driven medical imaging. I develop principled methods for building trustworthy AI systems that reason causally and generalize reliably to real-world clinical settings.

My current work involves multimodal prediction of treatment outcomes in diabetic macular edema using real-world electronic health records and optical coherence tomography. I also apply causal inference with multimodal foundation models to study anti-VEGF injection effects. Previously, I led the Causal Learning Group at Fudan University's Shanghai Key Laboratory of Intelligent Information Processing, and ranked 9th (solo) in the 2024 MIDRC XAI Grand Challenge on pneumonia diagnosis from chest radiographs.
